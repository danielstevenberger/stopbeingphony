Smartphone Addiction is Real

Yes, smartphone addiction is thriving. It strikes innocent victims as they spend countless hours texting, talking, gaming, surfing, posing and posting. In just a brief time, users can't imagine life without a phone; they become completely dependent. What they may not realize is that this compulsion is dangerous. Smartphones reduce willpower to nothing. The sanest of people become mindlessly preoccupied. Notifications must get checked immediately. Social media feeds are constantly refreshed. Phones receive more attention than reality; family, friends, and co-worker take a back seat. Dr. David Greenfield, the founder and medical director for the Center for Internet & Technology Addiction, warns that smartphone condition behavior. Without a doubt, smartphones affect mood and focus.
